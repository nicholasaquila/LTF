    Maintenance Release
    CASA Civil Aviation Regulation 43 format, matching the paper Maintenance Release exactly — kept here as a structural reference for the real, per-aircraft data below, not populated with any example values itself. Informed by CAAP 43-01 v2.3. Part 2 (Technical Log) has a real, working version further down for each aircraft — see below.
    A maintenance release stops being in force the moment any of these happens: an endorsement records the aircraft as not airworthy, the expiry date or hours are reached, a new release's inspection has begun, a Part 1 requirement wasn't completed by its due time, or a required airworthiness directive hasn't been actioned.
    Part 1 — Maintenance Requirements
    Lists maintenance that will fall due during this release's validity period, and when. This section doesn't certify completion itself — once something's done, it's transferred to Part 2 or the aircraft log book and certified there, then this row is marked complied-with.
    Item No. Maintenance Required Due at date/Aircraft TTIS Complied with, entered & certified in logbook or Part 2 of MR Date
    Part 3 — Daily Inspection Certifications and Aircraft Time-in-Service
    Dual purpose: the daily inspection must be signed for before the first flight of the day, and the day's total flight time plus the aircraft's running progressive total must be entered after the last flight, before it flies again. Same log also carries oil uplifts and any cycle counts the type requires — landings, pressurisation, engine starts, and similar.

    Fatigue & Duty

    Sourced directly from LTF's Fatigue Management System v5.0 (Oct 2022) — the FDP/flight-time limit tables (§1.8.5), cumulative limits (§1.8.6), and the Form 16 occurrence-report triggers (§1.7). A reference tool for tracking your own duty against these limits — not a replacement for the HOO/Safety Manager's own flight and duty records, and doesn't submit anything to LTF or CASA on its own.

    Occurrence Report — Form 16 (§1.7)
    Submit when: an FDP wasn't commenced or completed due to fatigue (including if completed only after a mitigating action like adding a crew member, reducing workload, or delaying reporting time); on reflection after an FDP, safety margins may not have been maintained; something in the environment could affect alertness (temperature, noise, accommodation); or an incident/event occurred where fatigue could have been a contributing factor. Must include your sleep history for the previous 72 hours.

    Scheduling [PREVIEW]

    Matches real student data against real live aircraft-booking conflicts and real instructor availability to suggest training slots. Suggest-only: nothing books itself — every match needs a person to hit Confirm. Search window reflects the real ~5-8 week booking lead time. The write path itself is real and built (createSingleStudentBooking, with every ID verified against real FlightLogger accounts) — but it's deliberately not wired to the Confirm button yet, pending an agreed way to test the very first real write safely. Confirming a real match right now tells you that plainly rather than pretending to succeed.
