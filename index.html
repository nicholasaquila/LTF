    Maintenance Release
    CASA Civil Aviation Regulation 43 format, matching the paper Maintenance Release exactly — structure only, not yet populated with data. Informed by CAAP 43-01 v2.3.
    A maintenance release stops being in force the moment any of these happens: an endorsement records the aircraft as not airworthy, the expiry date or hours are reached, a new release's inspection has begun, a Part 1 requirement wasn't completed by its due time, or a required airworthiness directive hasn't been actioned.
    Part 1 — Maintenance Requirements
    Lists maintenance that will fall due during this release's validity period, and when. This section doesn't certify completion itself — once something's done, it's transferred to Part 2 or the aircraft log book and certified there, then this row is marked complied-with.

    Part 2 — Endorsements
    Every defect found gets endorsed here, major or minor — the regulation doesn't distinguish, both must be recorded. A major defect (one that has caused or could cause a structural, control-system, engine, or fire failure) grounds the aircraft until it's rectified. A minor one may still allow flight if a pilot or LAME assesses it as not major and not required for the intended flight, with that assessment noted in the clearing column.

    Part 3 — Daily Inspection Certifications and Aircraft Time-in-Service
    Dual purpose: the daily inspection must be signed for before the first flight of the day, and the day's total flight time plus the aircraft's running progressive total must be entered after the last flight, before it flies again. Same log also carries oil uplifts and any cycle counts the type requires — landings, pressurisation, engine starts, and similar.

    Scheduling [PREVIEW]

    Matches real student data against real live aircraft-booking conflicts to suggest training slots. Suggest-only: nothing books itself — every match needs a person to hit Confirm. Real progress, real gaps: aircraft conflicts are checked against live FlightLogger data; instructor duty times are a fixed 08:00–17:00 placeholder, not yet real; and the write path (actually creating a booking on Confirm) isn't connected yet — that needs FlightLogger's booking-input shape verified first, which hasn't happened. Confirming a real match right now tells you that plainly rather than pretending to succeed.
    Suggested Matches
    No student, instructor, or availability data yet — nothing to match against. The matching logic itself is built and tested (see the Home page changelog), just waiting on real data to run against.
